Evaluation & Impact

A strong Work-Based Learning program is designed to include ongoing evaluation — not just to demonstrate results, but to guide program improvement over time. This page outlines the proposed approach.

Course project note: No evaluation data currently exists for this proposed program. This page reflects the evaluation framework being developed as part of graduate coursework and will be updated as the Evaluation and Assessment Plan is completed.

Possible areas of evaluation

  • Student growth in technical and employability skills
  • Student, family, and employer satisfaction and feedback
  • Participation and completion rates across pathway stages
  • Employer partner engagement and retention
  • Alignment of experiences to course and industry standards
  • Post-program outcomes (e.g., certification attainment, postsecondary or workforce transitions)

Why evaluation matters

Evaluation data is intended to help the program demonstrate value to students, families, employers, and district stakeholders, while identifying areas for continuous improvement. Findings may inform future presentations to school and community stakeholders, including a proposed Board of Education presentation.
