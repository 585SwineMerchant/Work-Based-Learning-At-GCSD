School-Based Enterprise

School-based enterprise is envisioned as the bridge between the classroom and the wider community — giving students real, supervised opportunities to apply culinary and business skills before moving into community or employer-based experiences.

Course project note: Specific enterprise activities, schedules, and operating procedures shown here are illustrative concepts. Actual school-based enterprise offerings would be determined through district approval and program planning.

What school-based enterprise may include

  • Student-run catering for school or community events
  • Pop-up food service events on or near campus
  • Café or concession-style operations connected to coursework
  • Community partnership events (farmers markets, district functions, local nonprofits)

Why it matters

School-based enterprise lets students apply technical skills (menu planning, food safety, production, service) alongside employability skills (teamwork, communication, resource management) in a supervised setting before transitioning to community or employer partnerships. It also strengthens ties between the school and the local community.
