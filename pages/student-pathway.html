Student Pathway

The proposed Culinary Pathway is designed to move students through a sequence of increasingly independent, supervised experiences — building skill and confidence at each stage before advancing to the next.

Course project note: Course sequencing, eligibility requirements, and certification pathways shown here are illustrative. Actual course offerings and requirements would be determined by district approval and the CTE program of study.

Proposed pathway progression

  1. Foundational coursework — nutrition, food safety and sanitation, kitchen fundamentals, and professional communication.
  2. Lab-based skill building — knife skills, cooking techniques, menu planning, and culinary math practiced in the school kitchen.
  3. School-based enterprise participation — contributing to catering, pop-ups, or school café/community events.
  4. Community & employer-connected experiences — job shadowing, mentoring, or supervised placements with local training partners, where established.

Skills you may build

Technical skills

Knife skills; food safety, sanitation, HACCP/FIFO; culinary math; menu planning and marketing; eggs, dairy, and produce prep; grains, pasta, potatoes, and legumes; poultry, meat, and seafood; bakery production; sustainable practices; and food safety certification (e.g., ServSafe or similar, where offered).

Employability skills

Attendance and punctuality, workplace appearance, quality of work, resource management, communication, thinking and problem solving, ethical and legal responsibilities, interpersonal dynamics, workplace foundations, and safety.

Possible pathway connections

Depending on final program design, the Culinary Pathway may connect to a 4+1 pathway option, a CTE endorsement, a national technical assessment, or postsecondary articulation opportunities. These connections are proposed concepts and would be confirmed through the district approval process.
