Employer Partners

Local foodservice and hospitality businesses are envisioned as training partners in the proposed Culinary Pathway — not simply providers of jobs. This page outlines what that partnership may involve.

Course project note: Partnership structures, agreements, and liability arrangements described here are conceptual. Actual employer partnership procedures would be established and approved through the district.

What it may mean to be a training partner

  • Hosting job shadowing, mentoring, or supervised student learning experiences
  • Providing guidance and feedback aligned to program learning objectives
  • Supporting the development of employability skills alongside technical skills
  • Collaborating with school staff on scheduling, supervision, and safety expectations
  • Participating in school-based enterprise events, guest instruction, or advisory input

The school's continued role

Even where students engage with an employer partner, the school is designed to remain responsible for communication, documentation, supervision coordination, safety protocols, and alignment of the experience to learning standards. Employers are not expected to manage these responsibilities independently.
