## Panel 1 — What Is Culinary WBL?

Work-Based Learning is structured, supervised, standards-aligned career
preparation — not simply students getting jobs.

Culinary WBL is designed to connect classroom instruction, kitchen labs,
school-based enterprise, and community and employer partnerships into one
supported pathway.

---

## Panel 2 — Existing Pathway Strengths

The Culinary Pathway isn't starting from zero. Students already:

- Earn ServSafe certification
- Visit local restaurants and hear from guest chefs
- Cater student and faculty events
- Plan, cost, and produce menus tied to coursework
- Collaborate with business classes on pricing and marketing

This proposal is designed to **formalize and expand** these strengths.

---

## Panel 3 — Student Pathway

Students move through a supported progression:

**Awareness → Preparation → Participation → Reflection**

Readiness is built through safety, professionalism, and technical skill —
before any more formal placement.

---

## Panel 4 — School-Based Enterprise as a Readiness Bridge

Catering, pop-ups, and food sales let students practice real production,
service, and customer-facing skills in a supervised school setting —
bridging the classroom and the community.

Rotating roles (production, service, sanitation, marketing, costing) support
**inclusive participation** for students with different strengths and needs.

---

## Panel 5 — Employer & Community Partners

Employers are envisioned as **training partners**, not just job sites.

An active culinary advisory board helps guide partnership growth — including
possible rotating catering opportunities identified through the board.

---

## Panel 7 — Evaluation & Impact

A strong WBL program needs evidence. Future evaluation is designed to
document student growth, employer feedback, safety, and program quality —
guided in part by the culinary advisory board.
