## Greece Culinary Work-Based Learning

### From Classroom Kitchen to Career

*A proposed partnership model for local employers and community partners*

---

### Why Partner?

- Support local workforce development
- Build early connections with motivated students
- Help shape student preparation
- Strengthen school-community relationships
- Create authentic career awareness for students

### Existing Pathway Strengths

The Culinary Pathway isn't starting from zero. It already includes in-house
catering, guest chef speakers, curriculum-tied menu planning and costing, and
an active culinary advisory board. Through advisory board connections,
roughly 5–6 real catering opportunities become available each year — a
potential source of authentic, rotating student production and service
experience as the program formalizes and expands.

### Ways to Participate

Guest speaker • career panel • site visit or facility tour host • job shadow
host • mock interview participant • resume feedback volunteer • skill
demonstration partner • advisory committee participant • internship or
cooperative experience partner, if approved

### What Students Are Learning

**Technical:** food safety & sanitation, knife skills, production, service,
menu planning
**Employability:** attendance, communication, teamwork, problem-solving,
professionalism

### Partnership Levels

Start wherever fits your capacity:

1. **Career Awareness** — guest speaking, classroom Q&A
2. **Career Exploration** — site visits, job shadows
3. **Skill Connection** — mock interviews, resume review, mentoring
4. **WBL Placement** — internships or supervised experiences, if approved

### What You Can Expect from the School

Clear communication • student preparation before participation • defined
learning goals • documentation and forms when applicable • respect for your
time and operations
