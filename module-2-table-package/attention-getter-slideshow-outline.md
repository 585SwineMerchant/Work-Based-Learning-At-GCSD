# Attention-Getter Slideshow Outline

A short, simple, loopable slideshow for a tablet or laptop at the
presentation table. Ten slides, designed to run on a timer (roughly 5–6
seconds per slide, ~50–60 second full loop) so it works as passive
attention-getting motion even when no one is actively reading it.

> Course project draft — proposed program, not official Greece Central School
> District policy.

## Design Notes

- One idea per slide, large type, high contrast (reuse the site's palette:
  deep green `#2E4630`, cream `#FAF8F4`, terracotta accent `#A5601C`)
- No autoplay audio — this runs silently at a busy table
- Loop continuously; slide 10 should flow naturally back into slide 1
- This outline is content/structure only — actual slide design/export is a
  separate step (see `module-2-package-checklist.md`)

---

### Slide 1 — Title

**Slide title:** Greece Culinary Work-Based Learning
**Key message:** From Classroom Kitchen to Career
**Suggested visual:** Title + tagline on a solid deep-green field, matching
the table covering
**Talking point:** "This is a proposed pathway connecting our culinary
classroom to real career experience."

### Slide 2 — What Is Culinary WBL?

**Slide title:** What Is Culinary WBL?
**Key message:** Structured, supervised, standards-aligned career prep —
not just students getting jobs.
**Suggested visual:** Simple 4-step arrow graphic: Classroom → Lab → SBE →
Employer Partnership
**Talking point:** "It's built around supervision and learning goals at
every stage, not just a placement."

### Slide 3 — Not Starting From Zero

**Slide title:** Building on Real Strengths
**Key message:** ServSafe, guest chefs, in-house catering, and curriculum-
tied menu planning already happen here.
**Suggested visual:** Short icon row (certificate, chef hat, tray, clipboard)
**Talking point:** "This isn't a new idea from nothing — it formalizes what
our program already does well."

### Slide 4 — Students Build Technical & Employability Skills

**Slide title:** Two Kinds of Skill
**Key message:** Knife skills and food safety, alongside communication and
teamwork.
**Suggested visual:** Two-column split: "Technical" / "Employability"
**Talking point:** "Every experience is designed to build both."

### Slide 5 — School-Based Enterprise as a Readiness Bridge

**Slide title:** School-Based Enterprise in Action
**Key message:** Catering and food sales give students real production and
service practice, safely, at school.
**Suggested visual:** Simple bridge graphic connecting "Classroom" to
"Community"
**Talking point:** "This is where students practice real deadlines and
customer service before an outside worksite."

### Slide 6 — Employers Become Training Partners

**Slide title:** Employers as Training Partners
**Key message:** Not just job sites — partners who help shape student
learning.
**Suggested visual:** Handshake or partnership icon, with "Training Partner"
label
**Talking point:** "An advisory board helps guide how local businesses get
involved."

### Slide 7 — Families Support Readiness and Reflection

**Slide title:** Families Are Part of the Process
**Key message:** Communication, encouragement, and reviewing expectations
together.
**Suggested visual:** Simple family/support icon
**Talking point:** "Families help students build the habits WBL depends on."

### Slide 8 — Inclusive Role Rotation

**Slide title:** A Role for Every Student
**Key message:** Production, service, sanitation, marketing, costing —
many roles, many strengths.
**Suggested visual:** Small grid of role icons (knife, tray, clipboard,
megaphone, calculator)
**Talking point:** "Because there are so many roles in a real event, this
works well for students with different needs and strengths."

### Slide 9 — Evaluation Helps the Program Improve

**Slide title:** Evidence, Not Just Intentions
**Key message:** Evaluation is designed to document growth and guide
improvement.
**Suggested visual:** Simple upward trend line or checklist icon
**Talking point:** "We want to know this works — and keep improving it."

### Slide 10 — Call to Action

**Slide title:** Scan the QR Code to Explore Resources
**Key message:** One website. Forms, pathway info, and more.
**Suggested visual:** Large QR code centered on a deep-green field, short URL
beneath it
**Talking point:** "Everything you just saw — and a lot more detail — is on
that site."
