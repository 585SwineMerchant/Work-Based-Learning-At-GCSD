## Greece Culinary Work-Based Learning

### From Classroom Kitchen to Career

*A proposed pathway for students and families*

---

### What Is Work-Based Learning?

Work-Based Learning (WBL) is structured, supervised, standards-aligned career
preparation — not simply students getting jobs. It's designed to connect
what students learn in the classroom and kitchen lab to real expectations in
foodservice, hospitality, and related careers.

### GCSD Culinary Already Prepares Students

The Culinary Pathway isn't starting from zero. Students already build
career-ready skills through:

- ServSafe certification
- Guest chef visits and restaurant trips
- In-house catering for school events
- Menu planning and costing tied to coursework
- Hands-on, scratch production and service

This proposal is designed to **formalize and expand** these existing
strengths into a more structured WBL system.

### What Students May Gain

- Career awareness and confidence
- Technical culinary skill practice
- Employability skills: communication, teamwork, problem-solving
- Resume and interview-ready experience
- A clearer path toward postsecondary or workforce options

### The Family Role

Families help by discussing career goals, encouraging attendance and
professionalism, reviewing expectations before any WBL experience, and
staying in communication with school staff. See the full **Family Support
Checklist** on the website.

### Possible Student Experiences

Guest speakers • career panels • site visits • job shadowing • school-based
enterprise & catering • mock interviews • resume preparation • internships
or cooperative experiences, if approved

### Readiness Expectations

Participation would be based on more than interest alone — things like
consistent attendance, safe kitchen behavior, and the ability to accept
feedback. Because production and service involve many different roles,
the pathway is designed to support **inclusive role rotation** for students
with different strengths, needs, 504 plans, or IEPs.
