Employer Questions & Answers

Greece Culinary Work-Based Learning — From Classroom Kitchen to Career

Is this the same as hiring an employee?

No. Employers are envisioned as training partners, not employers of record. The focus is on structured, supervised learning experiences — guest speaking, site visits, mentoring, or (at the most involved level) a supervised placement — not on filling a staffing need.

What supervision is required?

Students remain under real, active supervision at all times while on site, and the school stays responsible for communication, documentation, and safety coordination throughout. Employers are not expected to manage students unsupervised.

Is there any cost to participate?

No. There are no fees to participate at any partnership level. The only investment is your time — and you choose how much of it to give, from a single guest-speaking slot to an ongoing relationship.

What about liability and insurance?

These details have not yet been determined. Final liability, insurance, and worksite agreement requirements would be established through district approval and would need to follow Greece Central School District policy and New York State requirements before any placement-level partnership begins.

How much time does it take?

As little as one hour for a guest-speaking visit or classroom Q&A. Site visits and job shadows are typically a single scheduled block. Ongoing roles, like advisory committee membership or mentoring, are flexible and set by you.

Can we start small?

Yes — that's the design. The program is organized into four partnership levels — Career Awareness, Career Exploration, Skill Connection, and WBL Placement — so you can begin with something low-commitment and grow into a deeper relationship only if and when it makes sense for your business.
