Community Plan & Survey Design Summary

Greece Culinary Work-Based Learning

Why This Program Is Being Proposed

GCSD's culinary pathway is not a new idea — it has existed for approximately five years and already includes real production work through catering and school-based enterprise. Students already handle menu planning, costing, purchasing, preparation, and service for school and faculty events, building menus around the units they are currently studying.

The proposed WBL program is designed to extend that existing foundation into real industry settings, so students experience the pace, expectations, and responsibility of professional culinary work — not to replace what the classroom already does well.

Guiding Principles

  • Every experience should benefit three stakeholders at once: the student, the employer, and the school.
  • Funding is a support for growth, not the reason the program exists.
  • WBL is designed for a wide range of students — not a placement option limited to students who struggle in a traditional classroom.
  • Technical skill and employability skill are built together, deliberately, not left to chance.
Partner selection is about fit, not just willingness: a good employer partner understands this is an educational experience, communicates with the school, provides real supervision, and models professional expectations — not every willing worksite is automatically a good placement.

Stakeholder Surveys: What Was Designed

As part of program planning, two stakeholder surveys were designed to inform how the program should grow. These surveys have been designed but not yet distributed — this summary describes what they ask, not results, since none exist yet.

Community & Employer Survey

Asks local foodservice and hospitality employers about entry-level hiring needs, valued skills, capacity to host students, safety and supervision requirements, and what support from GCSD would make partnership easier.

Student Survey

Asks current and prospective culinary students about career interests, confidence levels, prior work experience, preferred WBL experience types, availability, and barriers like transportation that could affect participation.
