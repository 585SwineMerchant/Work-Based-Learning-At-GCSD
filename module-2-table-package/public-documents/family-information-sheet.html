Family Information Sheet

Greece Culinary Work-Based Learning — From Classroom Kitchen to Career

This one-page overview is designed as a leave-behind for families: what Work-Based Learning (WBL) is, the real course sequence your student may already be part of, what the program is designed to build, and what to expect as a family.

What Is Work-Based Learning?

WBL is a structured, supervised, standards-aligned approach that connects classroom instruction to real career-connected experiences. It is not simply a job — it is designed as a learning experience with defined objectives, supervision, and reflection, building both technical and employability skills.

The Culinary Pathway

Greece Central School District's culinary program follows a real, five-course sequence. Students build skill and readiness at each stage before moving toward more formal WBL experiences.

  • Strategies for Success — foundational habits, safety, and workplace readiness
  • Culinary 1 — core kitchen skills and food safety fundamentals
  • Culinary 2 — expanded technique, planning, and production
  • Advanced Culinary — higher-level production, service, and professionalism
  • Kitchen Management — leadership, costing, and operations

What This Program Is Designed to Build

Technical Skills

  • Food safety and sanitation practice
  • Kitchen and lab safety procedures
  • Production, prep, and service technique
  • Menu planning and food costing

Employability Skills

  • Attendance, punctuality, and professionalism
  • Communication and teamwork
  • Accepting and applying feedback
  • Reflection and goal-setting

Certifications & Potential College Credit

The pathway connects to ServSafe food safety certification and the YouScience Culinary 3 industry certification exam, with potential college credit through New York State ProStart articulation agreements with colleges including SUNY Cobleskill, Schenectady County Community College, and Tompkins Cortland Community College. Exact eligibility varies by institution.

How Families Are Kept Informed

The program is designed so families are told what a student is participating in, how it connects to coursework, and who to contact with questions — before and during any WBL experience.

What to Expect

Every student's path is different — because real production and service events involve many roles (production, service, sanitation, marketing, costing, and reflection), the program is designed to support students with a range of strengths, needs, 504 plans, or IEPs through varied roles alongside their peers.
