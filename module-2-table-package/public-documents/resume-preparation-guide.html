Resume Preparation Guide

Culinary Pathway · Greece Central School District

Many students build their very first resume as part of Culinary Work-Based Learning. This guide walks through what to include when school and kitchen experience — not paid work history — is most of what a student has to draw on.

Resume Sections, in Order

  • Contact Information — full name, phone number, email address (a simple, professional email — not a nickname or shared family account)
  • Objective or Summary — one or two sentences on the role you're seeking and what you bring to it
  • Education — school name, expected graduation year, Culinary Pathway coursework, and any certifications (ServSafe, etc.)
  • Relevant Experience — classroom kitchen labs, school-based enterprise/catering events, job shadows, volunteer work, or part-time jobs, even outside food service
  • Skills — a mix of technical kitchen skills and employability skills (see below)
  • References — "Available upon request" is acceptable for a first resume
No paid job yet? That's normal for a first resume. Classroom labs, catering events, ServSafe certification, and guest chef workshops are all legitimate experience — list them the same way you would list a job, with what you did and what you learned.

Two Kinds of Skills to List

Technical / Kitchen Skills

  • Knife skills & basic prep
  • Sanitation & food safety (ServSafe)
  • Following recipes & portioning
  • Line, plating, or service experience
  • Point-of-sale or cash handling

Employability Skills (11 CDOS)

  • Communication & teamwork
  • Punctuality & attendance
  • Problem solving under pressure
  • Following written & verbal directions
  • Professionalism & workplace appearance

Sample Entry: Turning a Class Experience into a Resume Line

Instead of: "Worked in Culinary 2 class."
Try: "Prepared and plated 40+ meals per shift in a classroom kitchen lab; maintained ServSafe sanitation standards and collaborated with a 4-person prep team."

Formatting Tips

  • Keep it to one page
  • Use a consistent, readable font (11–12pt)
  • List experience in reverse chronological order (most recent first)
  • Proofread carefully — ask a teacher or peer to review before submitting
  • Save and send as a PDF when applying online, unless a different format is requested
