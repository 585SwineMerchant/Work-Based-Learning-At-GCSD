Partner With GCSD Culinary

Help Build the Next Generation of Foodservice & Hospitality Talent

The Greece Central School District Culinary Pathway is proposing a formal Work-Based Learning program — and local employers are central to making it real. Partnering means becoming a training partner who helps students connect classroom learning to authentic workplace expectations, not simply a place students are sent to work.

Why Partner?

  • Support local workforce development in foodservice and hospitality
  • Build early relationships with motivated, trained students
  • Help shape what “job ready” means for your industry
  • Strengthen your business's connection to the community
  • Give back through a low-commitment, high-impact role

Four Ways to Get Involved

Every level is a real contribution — start wherever fits your capacity:

  • 1. Career Awareness — guest speaking, classroom Q&A, a short video message
  • 2. Career Exploration — hosting a site visit, tour, or job shadow
  • 3. Skill Connection — mock interviews, resume review, mentoring, menu feedback
  • 4. WBL Placement — hosting a supervised internship or co-op experience, if approved
What it costs you: your time — nothing else. There are no fees to participate. Most partners start with a single visit or one guest-speaking slot; the school handles scheduling, student preparation, and communication around the visit.

Students Bring Real Skills to the Table

GCSD culinary students work toward ServSafe food safety certification and the YouScience Culinary 3 industry credential, in a program aligned with the National Restaurant Association's ProStart curriculum. They already plan, cost, prepare, and serve real food through in-house catering and school events.
