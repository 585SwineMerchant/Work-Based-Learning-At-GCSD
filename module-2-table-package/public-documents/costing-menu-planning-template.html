Costing / Menu Planning Template

Culinary Pathway · Greece Central School District

Use this worksheet to plan a menu and calculate food cost per portion — the same market-order-form thinking used to plan real culinary purchases. List each ingredient As Purchased (AP), then total the cost to price the menu responsibly.

Cost Summary

Cost Per Portion = Total Food Cost ÷ Number of Portions

As a pricing benchmark, food cost typically runs about 28–35% of menu price in the food service industry. Remember to plan quantities using As Purchased (AP) amounts — the raw quantity bought — since trim and waste mean the Edible Portion (EP) yield is usually less than what was purchased.
