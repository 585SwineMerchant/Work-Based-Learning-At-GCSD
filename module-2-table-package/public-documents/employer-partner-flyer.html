Greece Culinary Work-Based Learning

From Classroom Kitchen to Career

Why Partner?

  • Support local workforce development
  • Build early connections with motivated students
  • Help shape student preparation
  • Strengthen school-community relationships
  • Create authentic career awareness for students

Existing Pathway Strengths

The Culinary Pathway isn't starting from zero. It already includes in-house catering, guest chef speakers, curriculum-tied menu planning and costing, and an active culinary advisory board. One board member currently contracts roughly 5–6 catering jobs a year — a potential source of authentic, rotating student production and service opportunities as the program formalizes and expands.

Ways to Participate

  • Guest speaker
  • Career panel or site visit host
  • Job shadow host
  • Mock interview / resume feedback
  • Advisory committee participant

What Students Are Learning

Technical: food safety & sanitation, knife skills, production, service, menu planning.

Employability: attendance, communication, teamwork, problem-solving, professionalism.

Partnership Levels

Start wherever fits your capacity:

  • 1. Career Awareness — guest speaking, classroom Q&A
  • 2. Career Exploration — site visits, job shadows
  • 3. Skill Connection — mock interviews, resume review, mentoring
  • 4. WBL Placement — internships or supervised experiences, if approved
This partnership model is designed to be advisory board-supported as it expands — the board helps review policies, employer connections, and WBL structures over time.

What You Can Expect from the School

Clear communication • student preparation before participation • defined learning goals • documentation and forms when applicable • respect for your time and operations
