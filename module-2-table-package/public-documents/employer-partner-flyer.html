Greece Culinary Work-Based Learning

From Classroom Kitchen to Career

Why Partner?

  • Support local workforce development
  • Build early connections with motivated students
  • Help shape student preparation
  • Strengthen school-community relationships
  • Create authentic career awareness for students

Existing Pathway Strengths

The Culinary Pathway isn't starting from zero. Students already work toward real, recognized credentials — ServSafe food safety certification and the YouScience Culinary 3 industry certification exam — through a program aligned with the National Restaurant Association's ProStart curriculum. It already includes in-house catering, guest chef speakers, curriculum-tied menu planning and costing, and an active culinary advisory board.

Through advisory board connections, roughly 5–6 real catering opportunities become available each year — a potential source of authentic, rotating student production and service experience as the program formalizes and expands.

The School's Continued Role

Even where students engage with an employer partner, the school remains responsible for communication, documentation, supervision coordination, safety protocols, and alignment of the experience to learning standards. Employers are not expected to manage these responsibilities independently.

Getting Involved

Greece Culinary Work-Based Learning

Ways to Participate

  • Guest speaker
  • Career panel or site visit host
  • Job shadow host
  • Mock interview / resume feedback
  • Advisory committee participant

What Students Are Learning

Technical: knife cuts, food safety and sanitation (HACCP, FIFO), culinary math and costing, menu planning, bakery production, meat/poultry/seafood preparation.

Employability: the 11 CDOS work-related skills — attendance, punctuality, workplace appearance, quality of work, resource management, communication, problem solving, ethical/legal responsibility, interpersonal dynamics, foundation development, and safety.

Partnership Levels

Start wherever fits your capacity:

  • 1. Career Awareness — guest speaking, classroom Q&A
  • 2. Career Exploration — site visits, job shadows
  • 3. Skill Connection — mock interviews, resume review, mentoring
  • 4. WBL Placement — internships or supervised experiences, if approved
This partnership model is designed to be advisory board-supported as it expands — the board helps review policies, employer connections, and WBL structures over time.

What You Can Expect from the School

Clear communication • student preparation • defined learning goals • documentation when applicable • respect for your time
