What Is Culinary Work-Based Learning?

From Classroom Kitchen to Career

A Structured Path, Not Just a Job

Work-Based Learning (WBL) is structured, supervised, standards-aligned career preparation — not simply students getting jobs. Culinary WBL is designed to connect classroom instruction, kitchen labs, school-based enterprise, and community and employer partnerships into one supported pathway that builds both technical and employability skills.

Existing GCSD Culinary Strengths

The Culinary Pathway is not starting from zero. Students already build career-ready experience through:

  • ServSafe certification
  • Guest chef speakers
  • Restaurant visits
  • In-house catering for school events
  • Curriculum-tied menu planning
  • Menu costing
  • Scratch production
  • Packaging and serving
  • Business-class collaboration on marketing materials
  • Advisory board connections

How WBL Formalizes and Expands These Strengths

The proposed WBL system is designed to formalize and expand this existing activity — documenting student growth, connecting internal culinary experiences to external employer and community partnerships, and using school-based enterprise as a readiness bridge toward more formal, supervised opportunities.

Greece Central School District's (GCSD) Culinary Pathway already operates real student-run production and service for school and faculty events — the proposed WBL system builds on that foundation rather than starting over.

The Pathway, Certifications & College Credit

From Classroom Kitchen to Career

The Culinary Pathway

GCSD's culinary pathway is a real, sequenced progression of courses:

  • Strategies for Success
  • Culinary 1
  • Culinary 2
  • Advanced Culinary
  • Kitchen Management

Real Certifications & College Credit

Students work toward ServSafe food safety certification and the YouScience Culinary 3 industry certification exam — and the pathway connects to real articulated college credit through New York State ProStart agreements with:

  • SUNY Cobleskill
  • Schenectady County Community College
  • Tompkins Cortland Community College
  • Morrisville State College
  • Niagara Falls Culinary Institute
  • The Culinary Institute of America
  • Culinary Institute of NY at Monroe College

Who Benefits

  • Students — career awareness, confidence, and resume-ready experience
  • Employers — early access to prepared, motivated students
  • Community — visible evidence of career-pathway quality
  • Families — clear expectations and communication
