Site Visit Checklist

For Employers Hosting a Tour or Job Shadow

A quick planning guide for hosting a culinary student site visit or job shadow — before, during, and after the visit. Keep this on hand with your point of contact for the day.

Before the Visit

Confirm date, arrival time, and total duration with the school Assign a staff point of contact for the day Plan a short safety and orientation briefing Identify what students will observe vs. do hands-on Note any PPE, dress code, or footwear requirements Confirm group size and any access/parking details with the school

During the Visit

Greet students and briefly introduce your team Deliver the safety briefing before entering work areas Provide real, active supervision at all times Issue PPE if students will be near equipment or hot areas Use structured activities or stations rather than open observation only Explain the “why” behind tasks, not just the “what” Leave time for student questions

After the Visit

Give students a brief closing debrief or takeaway Thank the group and note any standout students, if comfortable Share optional feedback with the school contact Flag any safety or logistics notes for next time Let the school know if you'd host again or want to go further
Even during an employer-hosted visit, the school remains responsible for communication, documentation, and alignment of the experience to learning standards — you are not expected to manage those parts independently.
