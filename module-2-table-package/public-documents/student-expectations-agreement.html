Student Expectations Agreement

Culinary Pathway · Greece Central School District

This agreement outlines what's expected of a student participating in Work-Based Learning, and what the student can expect from the program in return. Reviewing it together with a teacher or coordinator before a placement helps set clear expectations from the start.

As a Student, I Agree To:

  • Attend consistently and arrive on time and ready to work
  • Follow all safety and sanitation procedures at all times
  • Wear the required uniform or dress code for the setting
  • Communicate professionally with staff, supervisors, and customers
  • Follow written and verbal directions from supervising adults
  • Accept feedback and correction without argument
  • Notify my teacher/coordinator in advance if I will be absent
  • Keep student, employer, and customer information confidential
  • Complete any required reflection logs or hour tracking
  • Represent myself, my school, and the program with professionalism

In Return, the Program Will:

  • Provide a safe, supervised learning environment
  • Match experiences to my skills, interests, and any accommodations (504/IEP)
  • Give regular feedback on my performance and growth
  • Communicate with my family about my participation
  • Address any concerns I raise about safety or treatment promptly
If at any point a student feels unsafe, unfairly treated, or unable to meet these expectations due to a disability-related need, they should talk to their culinary teacher or WBL coordinator right away — this agreement is meant to support success, not create a barrier to it.
