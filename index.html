About the Proposal

A structured bridge from classroom to career

This site introduces a proposed Culinary Pathway Work-Based Learning (WBL) program for Greece Central School District. It is being developed as part of a graduate coursework project and is not yet an official district program. It is designed to illustrate how culinary students could move through a sequence of supervised, standards-aligned experiences — from classroom instruction and kitchen labs to school-based enterprise and, eventually, community and employer partnerships — while building both technical and employability skills. Final procedures, eligibility, and partnership agreements would be determined by district approval.

Program Vision

Career readiness through supervised experience

The proposed program envisions a continuum where classroom instruction, lab practice, school-based enterprise, and employer partnerships reinforce one another. Throughout, the school maintains responsibility for communication, documentation, supervision, safety, and alignment to learning standards. Work-Based Learning is designed as structured career preparation — not simply students taking on jobs.

Why Culinary WBL?

Technical skill meets employability skill

The culinary and foodservice industry offers accessible, high-demand entry points for students to practice both hands-on trade skills and durable employability skills — communication, teamwork, problem-solving, and professionalism — in real or near-real settings, supervised by instructors and, where established, employer training partners.
