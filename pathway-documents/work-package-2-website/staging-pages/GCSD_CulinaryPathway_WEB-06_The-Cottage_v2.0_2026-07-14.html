Explore step 4

The Cottage

The Cottage is the program's existing student-based enterprise: a place for authentic client-centered learning under teacher supervision.

Learning first

Why The Cottage exists

Instructional purpose

Students practice food safety, production planning, communication, service standards, and follow-through in work that has a real audience.

Client-centered performance

Selected class-based production and outside-of-class events can become evidence for Gateway 4 when the teacher confirms readiness.

Teacher authority

The classroom teacher determines whether an opportunity has enough instructional value and whether students are ready for assigned roles.

Preparation before independence

How roles are assigned

  • Role assignments are based on demonstrated readiness, safety, quality expectations, and instructional needs.
  • Students may rotate through preparation, production, service, planning, cleanup, and reflection responsibilities.
  • Outside-service goals never come before learning, supervision, safety, and manageable commitments.
Current boundary

Community requests

Community or school-based requests should use existing school or district communication channels until a monitored Cottage inquiry route is assigned. The public site should not collect event details, student information, payment information, or private records.
