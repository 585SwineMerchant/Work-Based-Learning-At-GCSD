Event Planning Tool

Intended user: Advanced Culinary and Kitchen Management students working with their teacher

Purpose and directions

Purpose: Plan a Cottage, pop-up, capstone, or course-connected culinary event from educational purpose through closeout.

When to use: Begin only after the teacher confirms the event is appropriate to explore. Complete sections in sequence and pause at each teacher approval point.

This public blank tool supports instruction. It is not a client agreement, purchasing authorization, food-safety approval, financial record, or promise that an event will occur.

7. Day-of quality checks

  • Team briefing completed; each person can explain the role and definition of done.
  • Ingredients, recipes, labels, allergens, and substitutions checked.
  • Equipment, utilities, holding/storage, sanitation, and emergency routes checked.
  • Production sequence and service deadline reviewed.
  • Quality checkpoints and escalation route reviewed.
  • Teacher retains final authority over safety, service commitments, finances, and changes.

8. Closeout and post-event review

  • Food, equipment, and spaces closed out according to teacher direction.
  • Unused ingredients and leftovers handled according to approved procedures.
  • Actual quantities, costs, waste, timing, and quality notes captured for class review.
  • Students complete individual event reflections.
  • Teacher determines which evidence, if any, is retained in approved school systems.
