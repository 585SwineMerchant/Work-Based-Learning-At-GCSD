Employer Safety and Supervision Overview

Intended user: Community participants, Cottage clients, guest contributors, and prospective future training stations

Purpose and directions

Purpose: Clarify safety, supervision, authority, privacy, and records expectations across current community activities and proposed senior work-based learning.

When to use: Use during early planning to identify which responsibilities belong to the teacher, district owner, community participant, mentor, or coordinator.

Download PDF

Current classroom, Cottage, and school-event activities

  • The teacher retains final authority for instruction, student readiness, safety, sanitation, allergens, equipment, grading, event commitments, accommodations, discipline, finances, and emergency decisions.
  • Guests and clients follow the approved scope, schedule, access, communication, privacy, and safety directions for the activity.
  • Students may practice leadership and client communication, but they do not replace staff authority, enter contracts, control funds, or make unapproved commitments.
  • A concern involving safety, student welfare, privacy, access, or an unexpected change is escalated immediately through the approved school route.

Proposed senior external work-based learning

  • A workplace could be used only after district authorization, site screening, agreement, mentor preparation, approved duties, and an individualized student training plan.
  • Daily workplace supervision would not replace coordinator oversight, school communication, site visits, check-ins, or course assessment.
  • Schedule, duties, mentor, or location could not change informally; an approved change process would be required.
  • Legal, insurance, labor-law, working-paper, transportation, staffing, supervision, incident-response, and records requirements remain unresolved until district review is complete.

Information that never belongs on a public page

  • Student names, schedules, contact information, medical or accommodation information, and placement details
  • Completed site reviews, agreements, training plans, hours logs, evaluations, incident reports, and change requests
  • Employer legal, insurance, financial, staffing, or personnel records
  • Private client dietary, payment, contract, or incident information

Use the approved route

This overview does not replace district safety, visitor, volunteer, facility-use, contracting, employment, or incident procedures. The authorized school or district owner must identify the process for a specific activity.
