Mercantile Capstone Participation Information

Intended user: Community members, employers, industry professionals, school groups, and prospective event participants

Purpose and directions

Purpose: Describe possible community participation in the planned Mercantile pop-up/capstone while keeping the event's unresolved details and approval boundaries clear.

When to use: Use for planning conversations only until the district confirms the final event title, structure, timing, scope, responsibilities, and participation routes.

Download PDF

A culminating learning event

The current concept connects culinary production, The Cottage, Kitchen Management leadership, quality control, event planning, career readiness, and post-event evaluation. Student learning and teacher supervision would govern every role.

Possible participation routes

RoutePossible contribution, subject to approval
Guest or industry participantShare career perspective, demonstrate an approved practice, join a structured feedback activity, or respond to student questions.
Client or audience connectionProvide a realistic need, audience, or service expectation that can be translated into an instructional task.
Event collaboratorSupport an approved part of planning, attendance, logistics, communication, or event delivery within clear staff direction.
Feedback contributorUse an approved prompt or rubric to provide specific feedback while the teacher retains assessment authority.
Sponsorship or material supportMay be considered only through district-approved procedures; this public resource does not solicit or authorize money, goods, services, or recognition terms.

What students may practice

  • Concept development, recipe testing, menu design, costing, production planning, scheduling, and role assignment
  • Production, service, quality control, professional communication, resource management, and problem-solving
  • Leadership through briefing, approved delegation, constructive feedback, closeout, and post-event evaluation

Boundaries that remain in place

  • The teacher retains authority for instruction, safety, grading, discipline, accommodations, finances, legal requirements, event commitments, and emergency response.
  • Students do not contract with participants, control funds, promise recognition, or independently change the plan.
  • Participation, access, visitor procedures, facility use, food safety, privacy, publicity, and records follow approved district processes.
  • The event concept does not create a senior WBL placement or training station.

Details still pending

Final event structure, timing, title, scope, participant roles, ownership, communication routes, and any sponsorship process require district confirmation. Interest does not reserve or guarantee a role.
