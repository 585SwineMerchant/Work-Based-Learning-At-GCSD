Proposed Senior WBL Final Presentation Planner

Intended user: Students, reviewers, and a future WBL coordinator

Purpose and directions

Purpose: Help a student build a concise final presentation that explains approved work, learning, evidence, feedback, growth, challenges, and next steps.

When to use: Use for planning, rehearsal, and future approved presentation review. The presentation would support but not independently determine course completion.

Privacy and permission check

  • Every image, logo, workplace name, customer detail, recipe, financial figure, and work product is approved for presentation.
  • No student medical, incident, personnel, customer, proprietary, or confidential information appears.
  • Claims about hours, credentials, duties, outcomes, and responsibilities match verified records.
  • The presentation describes learning rather than functioning as advertising for the workplace or program.

Rehearsal checklist

  • Opening explains the setting and approved role clearly.
  • Evidence is readable and connected to a specific claim.
  • Timing fits the future approved presentation limit.
  • Student can explain a challenge, feedback, improvement, and next step without reading every word.
  • Likely reviewer questions have been practiced.
  • Accessibility needs, captions, contrast, and alternate formats have been addressed.

Questions I expect and how I will answer

List likely questions about duties, learning, evidence, feedback, responsibility, and career direction.

Final revision notes

Record changes after rehearsal and the evidence still needing approval. A polished presentation does not replace verified hours, approved duties, course assignments, mentor feedback, coordinator review, or demonstrated competency.
