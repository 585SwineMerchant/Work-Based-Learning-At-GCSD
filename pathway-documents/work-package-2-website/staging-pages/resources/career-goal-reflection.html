Career Goal Reflection

Intended user: Culinary Pathway students

Purpose and directions

Purpose: Help students identify current interests, strengths, development needs, supports, and practical next steps.

When to use: Complete at the start of a planning period, after a new culinary experience, or before a teacher or counselor conference. Revisit it later to see what changed.

1. What parts of culinary or hospitality work interest you right now?

Consider cooking, baking and pastry, service, catering, nutrition, menu development, purchasing, management, entrepreneurship, hospitality, or another area.

2. What settings would you like to learn more about?

Examples: restaurant kitchen, bakery, institutional food service, hotel, catering, school nutrition, food manufacturing, entrepreneurship, or college/training lab.

3. What strengths do you bring?

Name technical skills and professional habits. Include evidence from a lab, event, course, job, volunteer role, or team experience.

4. Which skills or habits need more development?

Choose specific skills you can practice and explain why they matter.

5. Which credentials, assessments, or college/training opportunities interest you?

List opportunities you want to investigate. Do not mark anything as earned unless it is verified.

6. What barriers could affect your next step, and what support would help?

Consider schedule, transportation, cost, confidence, academic requirements, equipment access, family responsibilities, or information you still need.

7. What will you do in the next four to six weeks?

Name one realistic action, the person who can help, and the evidence that will show you completed it.

8. What is your longer-term direction?

Describe a possible next step after graduation. It can change as you learn more.
