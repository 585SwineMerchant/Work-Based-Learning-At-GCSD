Family Pathway and Course Overview

Intended user: Students, families, and school counselors

Purpose and directions

Purpose: Give families a direct view of the existing Culinary Arts program, its current courses and learning environments, and the proposed additions that remain under review.

When to use: Use when discussing course planning, student progress, pathway opportunities, or the difference between current coursework and proposed senior work-based learning.

Download PDF

Built on an existing program

GCSD Culinary Arts is an existing program. Current instruction includes Culinary Arts and Nutrition I and II, Advanced Culinary Arts, and Kitchen Management. The Cottage is the program's existing student-based enterprise.

Current course progression

Course or learning environmentCurrent role in the program
Culinary Arts and Nutrition IBuilds foundational safety, sanitation, production, organization, teamwork, and professional habits.
Culinary Arts and Nutrition IIExtends foundational technique, production responsibility, quality control, and career-connected practice.
Advanced Culinary ArtsDeepens technical work, authentic performance, credential and college-credit opportunities, planning, and readiness for greater responsibility.
Kitchen ManagementProvides a current leadership bridge that still includes culinary work, menu and recipe development, planning, execution, quality checks, and event leadership under teacher supervision.
The CottageProvides an existing client-centered school-based enterprise environment where learning, safety, readiness, and teacher supervision come before outside-service goals.

Annual scheduling details

Official course titles, credits, prerequisites, and the 2026-27 sequence should be confirmed through the school counseling and scheduling process. This public resource does not substitute for the current course catalog or student schedule.

How students demonstrate readiness

Students must successfully complete the relevant course. Course assessments, performance tasks, authentic work, reflection, feedback, and other verified evidence help the teacher determine what responsibility a student is prepared to take next. Attendance or seat time alone does not demonstrate competency.

Credentials, college credit, and graduation planning

Credential preparation and college-credit opportunities are connected to Culinary Arts coursework. Exact credential names, eligibility rules, college course mappings, registration steps, credits, and transcript details must be confirmed through approved school channels before a family relies on them for graduation or postsecondary planning.

Proposed organizing framework

The proposed Six Gateways and Culinary Passport would help organize verified evidence and clarify next steps. They would not replace course grades, create a public student record, or allow private progress information to be posted on the public website.

Proposed senior external work-based learning

A senior external work-based learning course with a proposed 300 verified hours remains under review and is not active. Authorization, calendar, staffing, legal and insurance requirements, working papers, transportation, training-station approval, supervision, and records workflow must be resolved before it can be offered.
