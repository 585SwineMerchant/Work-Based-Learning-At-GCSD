Family Student-Support Checklist

Intended user: Students and families

Purpose and directions

Purpose: Help families support attendance, preparation, professional habits, communication, planning, transportation questions, and appropriate records boundaries.

When to use: Use at the start of a course, before a major project or Cottage event, or during a family discussion about progress and next steps.

Attendance and preparation

  • We know the current attendance, punctuality, and make-up expectations communicated by the teacher.
  • The student plans enough time to arrive prepared for class, lab, or an approved event.
  • We have reviewed the current course requirements for clothing, footwear, hygiene, equipment, and materials.
  • The student communicates an absence, delay, or preparation problem through the expected school route.

Professional habits

  • The student can explain the assigned role, deadline, safety expectations, and definition of done.
  • The student practices reliable communication, teamwork, organization, cleanup, and response to feedback.
  • The student asks for help when a task, safety condition, or responsibility is unclear.
  • We discuss specific evidence of growth rather than relying only on attendance or interest.

Planning and next steps

  • We have confirmed the official course title, credit, prerequisite, and schedule through school channels.
  • Any credential or college-credit plan uses verified names, eligibility, registration, credit, and transcript information.
  • The student has one realistic short-term action and knows who can help.
  • Resume, reflection, costing, schedule, or event-planning tools are completed only when directed and stored through approved systems.

Transportation and communication

  • We follow the transportation instructions for the specific approved school activity and do not assume an arrangement.
  • Questions use the teacher, counseling, administrative, or monitored pathway contact route.
  • Private student, medical, dietary, accommodation, incident, hours, evaluation, or agreement information is never posted or sent through a public page.

Proposed senior external work-based learning

The proposed senior course and 300-hour structure are not active. This checklist is not an application, transportation agreement, family acknowledgment, or placement request.
