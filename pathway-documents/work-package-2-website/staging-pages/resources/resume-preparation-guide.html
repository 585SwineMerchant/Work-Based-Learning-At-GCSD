Resume Preparation Guide

Intended user: Culinary Pathway students

Purpose and directions

Purpose: Help students turn verified coursework, labs, Cottage work, credentials, and leadership into accurate resume evidence.

When to use: Use when building or revising a first resume for employment, a program application, college or training, a portfolio, or an interview.

What a resume does

A resume gives a reader a concise record of what you have learned, practiced, completed, and contributed. It should be accurate, easy to scan, and tailored to the opportunity. School-based culinary experience belongs on a first resume when it is described honestly.

Recommended sections

  • Contact information: full name, professional email address, and a reliable phone number.
  • Summary or objective: one or two sentences naming the opportunity and the strengths you bring.
  • Education: school, expected graduation date, Culinary Arts coursework, and relevant academic achievements.
  • Relevant experience: labs, The Cottage, pop-up events, community service, employment, job shadowing, clubs, and leadership assignments.
  • Credentials and college credit: list only completed credentials, passed assessments, or college-credit participation you can verify.
  • Skills: combine technical culinary skills with employability skills such as communication, teamwork, reliability, and problem-solving.

Culinary-specific action verbs

Prepared, measured, scaled, fabricated, baked, produced, plated, served, sanitized, organized, coordinated, costed, scheduled, tested, monitored, coached, communicated, resolved, evaluated, and improved.

Weak versus strong descriptions

WeakStronger and more specific
Worked in culinary class.Prepared assigned menu items in team labs while following standardized recipes, sanitation routines, and production deadlines.

Describe only what you actually did

Accuracy check

Do not claim a credential, college course, leadership role, number of meals, dollar result, worksite placement, or Gateway completion unless you can verify it. Replace bracketed examples with your own accurate details.

Formatting guidance

  • Keep a student resume to one page unless an application requires more.
  • Use one readable font at 10.5-12 points and consistent headings.
  • Use reverse chronological order within experience and education sections.
  • Begin experience bullets with action verbs and include specific, verified detail.
  • Use a professional file name such as Firstname_Lastname_Resume.pdf.
  • Save as PDF for submission unless another format is requested.

Proofreading checklist

  • Name, phone number, and email are correct.
  • Course names, credential names, dates, and employer or event names are accurate.
  • Verb tense is consistent: present tense for current work and past tense for completed work.
  • Spelling, capitalization, punctuation, and spacing are clean.
  • Every statement is something I can explain in an interview.
  • A teacher, counselor, family member, or trusted reviewer has read the final version.
