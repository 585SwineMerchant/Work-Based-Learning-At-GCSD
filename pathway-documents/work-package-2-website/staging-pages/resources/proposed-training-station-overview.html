Proposed Senior WBL Training-Station Overview

Intended user: Employers and organizations considering a possible future training-station role

Purpose and directions

Purpose: Explain the educational purpose, approval sequence, mentor role, and unresolved conditions for a possible senior work-based learning training station.

When to use: Use for review and planning only. The senior course, training-station system, and public interest route are not active.

Download PDF

Learning before labor

A future training station would be part of an authorized high-school course, not a staffing service. The student would remain a student first, and approved learning objectives would govern the experience.

What a training station would be

A restaurant, hospitality, food-service, or related workplace approved to provide supervised practice aligned with an individual student training plan. It would serve students who have already demonstrated appropriate preparation inside the school program.

What approval would require

StageRequired review before use
InterestAn organization begins a planning conversation through a monitored route. Interest is not approval or a placement guarantee.
Site and role reviewThe coordinator reviews learning value, workplace conditions, supervision, safety, accessibility, schedule, location, duties, mentor capacity, and records needs.
District decisionThe correct district owners complete legal, insurance, labor-law, working-paper, transportation, staffing, fiscal, policy, and authorization review.
Agreement and mentor preparationOnly an approved agreement, trained mentor, approved duties, communication route, and incident/escalation process could support launch.
Student-specific planA student would need an approved placement record and individualized training plan before the first counted hour.
Ongoing oversightCoordinator check-ins, site visits, schedule and duty controls, hours verification, feedback, and evaluation would continue through the experience.

Division of responsibility

  • The district and coordinator would set course requirements, learning objectives, approved records, and placement conditions.
  • The workplace mentor would supervise daily approved duties, communicate with the coordinator, observe performance, and provide feedback.
  • The mentor would not independently expand duties, location, schedule, or supervision outside the approved plan.
  • The student would follow the training plan, workplace expectations, safety procedures, communication requirements, and school course requirements.

Records and public boundary

Training-station profiles, site reviews, agreements, mentor acknowledgments, student plans, schedules, hours, evaluations, incidents, and change requests would be restricted records in approved district systems. None should be completed or submitted through a public webpage.

Not active

The proposed senior course and 300-hour structure are not authorized for implementation. No training-station approval or student placement can be created from this overview.
