Six Gateways and Culinary Passport

The Gateway and Passport framework would organize evidence from current courses and authentic work so students, families, and teachers can see progress and next steps.

How progress is recorded

Instruction, evidence, Gateway, Passport

  1. Instruction and practice. Students learn and practice skills in Culinary Arts courses, labs, production routines, The Cottage, and leadership assignments.
  2. Assessed evidence. Teachers evaluate skills through course assessments, laboratory performance, authentic work, reflection, observation, and other documented evidence.
  3. Gateway completion. When required evidence and course expectations are met, the corresponding Gateway is completed.
  4. Passport record and next step. Completed Gateways are recorded in the private Culinary Passport, which shows current position, completed evidence, and the next step.

Course completion and demonstrated competency work together. The Passport is not a substitute for grades. It helps teachers understand learning previously completed, including when a student moves between teachers, courses, Arcadia, and Olympia.

Readiness markers

The six Gateways

Gateway 1

Cleared for supervised kitchen participation: safety, sanitation, hygiene, emergency procedures, basic equipment safety, and professional expectations.

Gateway 2

Demonstrated kitchen-work readiness: mise en place, station organization, recipe interpretation, measuring, scaling, knife skills, time management, team communication, kitchen systems, and cleanup.

Gateway 3

Demonstrated foundational culinary competence: culinary techniques, ingredient knowledge, product quality, and consistent instructional performance.

Gateway 4

Demonstrated client-centered culinary performance through Advanced Culinary and The Cottage: authentic production, client expectations, event execution, quality control, and service.

Gateway 5

Professional, leadership, and career readiness through Kitchen Management: planning, delegation, communication, quality, safety, resource management, and career preparation.

Gateway 6

External WBL readiness and completion, available only if the senior external WBL course and placements are authorized.

Passport privacy

Private records, increasing responsibility

Completed Gateways may support increasing responsibility inside the program. The student-facing phrase Travel Rights can describe program readiness for responsibility beyond the classroom, but it does not grant employment, placement, working papers, or legal authorization.

Passport records are private educational records. Any future external WBL participation would still require separate district approval, placement approval, family communication, legal documentation, and approved records systems.
