The Competency Framework

Six Gateways and the Culinary Passport

Students would earn progressively greater opportunities by demonstrating competency — not by finishing a course or completing paperwork.

This page explains how the proposed Gateway and Passport system is designed to work. It describes the framework itself, not any individual student's record — Passport records are private and restricted (see below).

Framework Status

The Pathway plans to use a six-Gateway competency framework and a Culinary Passport record; these are in development. Neither the Six Gateway Model nor the Culinary Passport has been formally adopted by GCSD. Gateways 1 through 5 are documented as an "Approved for Planning Use" internal rubric ready for staff review; Gateway 6 is a Conditional Planning Draft (see below).

The Record

What the Culinary Passport is

The Culinary Passport is designed to be the student's official competency record. It would document demonstrated mastery rather than course completion, serving as a:

  • Competency record
  • Progression record
  • Articulation document
  • Instructional planning document
  • Readiness verification system
  • Student motivation system

The governing rule for the Passport is direct: it would record verified competency, not attendance, seat time, participation alone, completion of paperwork, curriculum exposure, possession of a credential, or completion of an assessment without demonstrated learning.

Passport Records Are Private

Individual student Passport records are restricted student records. They are never published on this public website. This page explains how the Passport system is designed to work in general — it does not, and will not, display any student's actual Gateway status, evidence, or record. Once a role-gated Participant Resource Center exists, a student's own record would be accessible only to that student, their family, and authorized staff.

The Framework

The six Gateways

Each Gateway represents a specific, verified competency — earned in a specific learning environment.

Proposed

Gateway 1 — Cleared for Supervised Kitchen Participation

Earned through: Culinary 1.

Safety, sanitation, food safety, personal hygiene, emergency procedures, basic equipment safety, and professional expectations. Every critical safety criterion must be demonstrated before supervised kitchen participation begins — this threshold cannot be averaged away by strong performance elsewhere.

Proposed

Gateway 2 — Demonstrated Kitchen-Work Readiness

Earned through: Culinary 1 and Culinary 2.

Mise en place, station organization, recipe interpretation, measuring and scaling, knife skills, time management, team communication, kitchen systems, and cleanup/closing procedures — demonstrated dependably across more than one task, not a single strong lab.

Proposed

Gateway 3 — Demonstrated Foundational Culinary Competence

Earned through: Culinary 1 and Culinary 2 (cumulative).

Culinary techniques, ingredient knowledge, product quality, and consistent instructional performance. Represents readiness to enter Advanced Culinary — earned throughout the full course sequence, not from a single assessment event.

Proposed

Gateway 4 — Demonstrated Client-Centered Culinary Performance

Earned through: Advanced Culinary and The Cottage.

Authentic production, catering, client expectations, event execution, quality control, and professional service — the ability to plan, execute, communicate, control quality, serve, and close work when a real client, audience, deadline, or consequence is present.

Proposed

Gateway 5 — Professional, Leadership, and Career Readiness

Earned through: Kitchen Management (a proposed course — not yet part of the course catalog).

Leadership, kitchen management, supervision, employment preparation, résumé, portfolio, interview skills, and career readiness. Completion would earn External WBL Educational Readiness — the student-facing term for this is Travel Rights. Travel Rights confirm educational readiness only; they do not guarantee a specific employer, schedule, transportation arrangement, support plan, or placement.

Conditional

Gateway 6 — Completed Authentic Workplace Learning

Earned through: the proposed Senior Work-Based Learning Course (1.0 credit, Grade 12).

300 verified workplace hours, a completed portfolio, employer evaluation, coordinator evaluation, and a final presentation — representing successful transfer of pathway competencies into professional practice.

Conditional Planning Draft

Conditional planning draft. Not adopted district policy. Not authorized for implementation. Gateway 6 cannot currently be completed by any student. The Culinary Passport's Gateway 6 tab exists so the record structure is ready, but no Gateway 6 completion may be recorded while this authorization remains conditional.

The Decision

Completion levels

Every Gateway decision would use these three levels — not a percentage average across unrelated standards.

Completion with Distinction

The body of evidence consistently exceeds pathway expectations, with no critical standard below Completion.

Completion

The body of evidence meets pathway expectations, including every critical standard.

Not Yet Completed

The required competency has not yet been demonstrated, or essential evidence is incomplete. Additional instruction and reassessment are required; no Passport completion is awarded until competency has been demonstrated.

When a Student Isn't There Yet

Reassessment

A "Not Yet Completed" result is designed to lead to a specific next step, not a dead end. Students would be able to reassess after:

  • Feedback
  • Reflection
  • Additional practice
  • Remediation

Immediate retesting would not be permitted. An equivalent assessment — measuring the same competency through a fresh but comparable task — would be used instead, and the new result is recorded without erasing the history of support and growth that led to it.

Staying Current

Reverification

Competencies would remain part of a student's permanent record, but critical competencies — especially safety — would require periodic reverification when a student transitions into a more advanced learning environment. The design calls for reverification at five points:

  • Before client-centered production through The Cottage
  • Before Kitchen Management leadership or supervisory responsibility
  • Before external Work-Based Learning begins
  • Before any new high-risk duty or unfamiliar equipment
  • After a significant safety incident or extended absence
