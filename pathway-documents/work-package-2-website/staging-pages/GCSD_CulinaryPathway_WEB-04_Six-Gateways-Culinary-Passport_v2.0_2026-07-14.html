Six Gateways and Culinary Passport

The proposed Passport would help students see where they are, what evidence is complete, what support is needed, and what comes next.

How readiness is shown

The evidence chain

  1. 1

    Instruction and course requirements

    Students learn and practice through the course sequence. Passing the relevant course remains required.

  2. 2

    Assessments and authentic performance

    Teachers review course assessments, kitchen performance, client-centered work, professional habits, and other evidence.

  3. 3

    Verified competency

    Readiness is confirmed through evidence, not attendance or seat time alone.

  4. 4

    Gateway record and next step

    The Passport would record the student's current position and identify the next opportunity or support.

Readiness markers

The six Gateways

1. Supervised kitchen participation

Earliest opportunity during introductory instruction after safety, sanitation, and basic participation expectations are met.

2. Kitchen-work readiness

Demonstrated through repeated safe, reliable kitchen habits during introductory coursework.

3. Foundational culinary competence

Built through Culinary Arts and Nutrition I/II and maintained through later courses.

4. Client-centered culinary performance

Demonstrated through Advanced Culinary Arts and The Cottage when students are ready for authentic production.

5. Leadership and career readiness

Demonstrated through Kitchen Management leadership, continued culinary practice, and readiness to represent the program beyond the classroom.

6. Authentic workplace learning

Would require verified senior WBL hours and completed evidence after the senior course is authorized.

Records boundary

Passport privacy

Passport records are private student records. A public site may explain the system, but completed Passports, Employability Profiles, evaluations, hours, placement records, incidents, and agreements belong only in approved restricted district systems.
