Explore step 9

Results and Accountability

The connected framework has no longitudinal results yet, but the underlying Culinary Arts program has current activity that can be described carefully.

Three lenses

What can be reviewed

Student learning

Course completion, competency evidence, credentials, leadership readiness, and student reflection can show growth without exposing private records.

Learning-environment quality

Teachers can review whether classroom kitchen, The Cottage, leadership work, and any future WBL settings provide safe, meaningful learning.

Program quality

The program can examine access, course sequence, credential opportunities, advisory feedback, partner readiness, and resource usefulness.

Small and useful

Public reporting

A future annual public update should use a small number of meaningful measures. It can report aggregated or de-identified information such as course offerings, participation counts when approved, credential opportunities, enterprise activity, and major improvements. It should not create teacher busywork or expose student records.

Use what already exists

Internal evidence

  • Course assessments and teacher-verified performance evidence.
  • Passport and Employability Profile information in restricted systems.
  • The Cottage event review, client feedback, and instructional reflection where approved.
  • Senior WBL hours, evaluations, mentor feedback, and incidents only if the course is authorized and records workflow is approved.
