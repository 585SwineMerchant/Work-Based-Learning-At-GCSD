Employers and Community Partners

Community participation can support culinary learning in several ways, from simple career exposure to possible future training stations.

Participation routes

Several ways to connect

General community connection

Guest speaking, demonstrations, career conversations, site visits, industry feedback, and classroom connections can support student learning without creating a placement.

Cottage client request

Approved school or community requests may become Cottage learning experiences when they fit the course, schedule, resources, and student readiness.

Mercantile capstone support

Community partners may support the culminating Mercantile pop-up/capstone through guest participation, feedback, event support, collaboration, attendance, or sponsorship only where allowed.

Proposed senior WBL training station

A restaurant, hospitality, food-service, or related partner could become a senior WBL training station only after district authorization, screening, legal/risk review, and placement approval.

Interest to approval

A clear approval path

  1. Interest. A community member, employer, or school group expresses interest through a monitored route.
  2. Screening. The program checks fit, supervision, safety, learning value, schedule, and resource needs.
  3. Approval. The correct district owner confirms what is allowed before any commitment is made.
  4. Delivery and review. The activity is carried out, documented appropriately, and reviewed for student learning value.
Records boundary

No restricted records on public pages

Partner agreements, site reviews, completed evaluations, hours records, incident records, and student records belong in approved restricted district systems, not public pages.
