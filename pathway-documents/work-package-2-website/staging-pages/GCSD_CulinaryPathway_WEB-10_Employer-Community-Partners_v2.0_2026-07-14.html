Explore step 8

Employers and Community Partners

Community partners can strengthen student learning, but external training-station participation must be screened, approved, supervised, and documented through district processes.

Do not collapse them

Three different routes

General community connection

A guest speaker, tour, advisory conversation, or classroom connection may support learning without becoming a training station.

The Cottage request

A school or community request for student-prepared work belongs with the teacher-supervised enterprise process.

Senior WBL training station

A future external placement site would require screening, approval, orientation, a training plan, and restricted records after the course is authorized.

Future process

How interest becomes approval

  1. 1

    Interest

    A business or organization indicates the kind of learning opportunity it may be able to support.

  2. 2

    Screening and site review

    The program checks student-learning value, safety, supervision, schedule, duties, and district requirements.

  3. 3

    Approval and orientation

    A mentor receives expectations before any student begins approved work.

  4. 4

    Training plan and review

    Student duties, hours, evidence, communication, and concerns are documented in restricted systems.

Clear boundaries

Authority and records

  • Employers supervise daily workplace tasks but do not set curriculum or determine final course evaluation.
  • Specific partner reviews, agreements, training plans, hours, evaluations, and incidents are restricted records.
  • A public interest form should not go live until ownership, monitoring, privacy, and approval workflow are assigned.
