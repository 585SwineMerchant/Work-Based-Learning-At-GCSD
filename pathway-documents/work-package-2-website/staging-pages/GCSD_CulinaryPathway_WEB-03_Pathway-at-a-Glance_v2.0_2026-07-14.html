Pathway at a Glance

The Culinary Pathway builds from existing Culinary Arts coursework and enterprise activity toward a clearer system for progress, readiness, credentials, college credit, and career-connected learning.

Program structure

What exists and what comes next

Existing Culinary Arts program

Students already learn through Culinary Arts and Nutrition I and II, Advanced Culinary Arts, Kitchen Management, kitchen labs, credential preparation, college-credit opportunities, and The Cottage.

Gateway and Passport framework

The proposed framework would organize evidence from current instruction, assessment, and authentic work so progress can be understood consistently across teachers, courses, Arcadia, and Olympia.

Senior external WBL addition

The proposed senior course would add supervised external workplace learning after district authorization, approved placements, staffing, legal/risk review, transportation decisions, and records workflow are complete.

Graduation and readiness

Clear organization supports real decisions

A clearer pathway helps students understand where they are, families understand opportunities, teachers maintain continuity, and new teachers enter the program without forcing students to begin again. It also supports district consistency across Arcadia and Olympia.

The structure connects course completion, assessed skill, authentic performance, credential opportunities, college credit, graduation planning, and career readiness without treating those records as interchangeable.
