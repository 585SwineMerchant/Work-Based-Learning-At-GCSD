Explore step 7

Senior External Work-Based Learning

The senior external WBL course is a proposed 1.0-credit culmination, separate from the existing Culinary Arts program and not currently an available placement program.

Proposed senior addition

Authorization boundary

Authorization required

The senior external Work-Based Learning course remains a conditional proposal. It requires district authorization, staffing, legal and insurance review, transportation decisions, approved records workflow, and current operating procedures before students or employers can use it.

What it would include

Draft course model

Eligibility

Students would need successful course completion, demonstrated readiness, and teacher/program approval before representing the pathway externally.

300 verified hours

The draft model uses a 300-hour planning benchmark, roughly 10 hours per week across 30 active workweeks. The final calendar and weekly expectation require confirmation.

Training plan and supervision

A student would need a clear training plan, mentor expectations, program oversight, regular check-ins, and restricted records.

Portfolio and final presentation

Workplace learning would be documented through approved evidence and a culminating presentation or review.

Before use

What authorization still needs

  • Course approval, credit, calendar, enrollment rules, and sequence.
  • Staffing, coordinator authority, mentor orientation, and supervision procedures.
  • Legal, labor-law, insurance, transportation, compensation, and safety review.
  • Restricted workflow for hours, evaluations, agreements, incidents, and student records.
