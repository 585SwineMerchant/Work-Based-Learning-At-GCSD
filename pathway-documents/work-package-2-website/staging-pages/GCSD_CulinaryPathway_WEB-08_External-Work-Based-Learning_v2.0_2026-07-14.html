Senior External Work-Based Learning

The senior external work-based learning course is a developed proposal that would extend the pathway into approved workplace settings after district authorization.

Authorization required

Developed proposal, not active placement

The proposed model is not merely an undeveloped idea. Planning materials have been drafted for student interest and readiness, training plans, employer participation, supervision, hours verification, coordinator check-ins, portfolio work, employer feedback, final presentation, and program evaluation.

The course still requires district authorization, staffing, legal/risk review, placement procedures, records systems, transportation decisions, employer screening, and final approval before students can participate.

Draft course model

What it would include

Credit and hours

The planning model uses a proposed 1.0-credit, 300-hour structure. Weekly equivalents should be published only after the actual calendar and course sequence are confirmed.

Training plan and supervision

Each placement would need approved learning goals, a qualified training station, active supervision, coordinator check-ins, and clear boundaries for duties and safety.

Portfolio and final presentation

Students would document learning, reflect on performance, gather evidence, and complete a final presentation tied to readiness and career planning.

Public boundary

No active forms or placements yet

Blank proposed resources may be opened for review from Program Resources. They are not active submission routes, and they do not create approval, placement, transportation, employment, or legal authorization.
