Courses and Learning Environments

Students progress through required courses where teachers assess skill, safety, professionalism, and readiness for greater responsibility.

Courses

Current sequence, carefully stated

Culinary Arts and Nutrition I/II

Existing introductory culinary instruction. Exact 2026-27 titles, credits, grade placement, prerequisites, and building availability require confirmation against the official course guide.

Advanced Culinary Arts

Existing advanced instruction that may include career research, ServSafe preparation, client-centered production, pop-up or event work, and management/service learning.

Kitchen Management

A leadership bridge that continues culinary work through recipe testing, menu development, scheduling, delegation, quality control, coaching, debriefing, and capstone preparation. Exact implementation details require Jason/Corey confirmation.

Pacing remains teacher-controlled. Planning outlines can show an order of learning objectives, but they should not be presented as a fixed week-by-week calendar unless the classroom teacher confirms that sequence.
Learning environments

Where learning happens

Classroom kitchen

Students build food safety, knife skills, mise en place, production habits, teamwork, and professional communication under teacher supervision.

The Cottage

The existing student-based enterprise gives students authentic client-centered work when the teacher determines the opportunity has instructional value.

Leadership and external settings

Kitchen Management develops internal leadership. Senior external WBL would add off-site workplace learning only after authorization.

Progression

Course completion and readiness

  • Students must pass the relevant course to move forward in the sequence.
  • Course assessments, authentic performance, teacher observation, and other verified evidence demonstrate readiness for additional responsibility.
  • Gateway evidence clarifies progress; it does not replace successful course completion.
