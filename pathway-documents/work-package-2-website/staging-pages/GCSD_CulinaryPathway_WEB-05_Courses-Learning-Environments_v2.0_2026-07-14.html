Courses and Learning Environments

Students build skill through a sequence of Culinary Arts courses, assessed food laboratories, production routines, enterprise work, leadership practice, and proposed senior external work-based learning.

Current courses

Culinary Arts and Nutrition I and II

Culinary Arts and Nutrition I and II develop the daily habits and technical base students need before taking on more complex production. The documented sequence begins with safety, sanitation, hygiene, foodborne-illness prevention, emergency procedures, food flow, temperature control, HACCP concepts, safe storage, and contamination prevention.

Students then build kitchen procedures: measuring, standardized recipes, mise en place, smallwares and commercial equipment, knife identification and knife safety, station setup, cleanup, teamwork, and professional behavior. Assessed labs develop baking fundamentals, grains, pasta, nutrition, allergens, flavor, kitchen math, costing, menu pricing, and foundational food-preparation methods.

Pacing remains teacher-controlled. The scope and sequence provides the learning progression; teachers adjust timing to class needs, available events, facilities, and student readiness.

Advanced Culinary

Advanced production and client-centered work

Advanced Culinary moves students into higher-level production, restaurant and food-service industry study, kitchen operations, customer service, management concepts, communication, professionalism, and production planning. Students continue safety and sanitation, standardized recipes, workstation setup, knife skills, baking, soups, sauces, vegetables, starches, salads, sandwiches, poultry, meat, seafood, dairy, eggs, and evaluated food labs.

The course also connects to The Cottage, pop-up and enterprise activity, recipe research and testing, menu development, costing, client-centered work, service planning, management learning, credential preparation, and college-credit opportunity where the district confirms eligibility.

Leadership course

Kitchen Management

Kitchen Management exists as the leadership bridge after Advanced Culinary. Culinary work continues: students maintain and apply cooking skills while focusing on leadership, planning, recipe testing, menu development, quality control, production organization, event preparation, career readiness, and support for less-experienced classmates.

Exact 2026-27 implementation details, including final title, credit, schedule, grade placement, and event responsibilities, still require Jason/Corey confirmation.

Course completion

Evidence and readiness

Students must pass the relevant course. Assessments, laboratory performance, authentic production, reflection, and teacher observation provide evidence of what students can do.
