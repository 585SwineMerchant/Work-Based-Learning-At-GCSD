Explore Step 5

Leadership and Kitchen Management

Kitchen Management gives students a place to keep cooking while practicing leadership, quality control, event planning, and career readiness.

Leadership through culinary practice

Cooking, planning, and supporting others

Recipe testing and skill maintenance

Students continue culinary production, test recipes, maintain technique, and use food quality as part of leadership practice.

Menu and event planning

Students help plan menus, production schedules, service flow, costing, purchasing needs, and event execution.

Quality control and coaching

Students support younger or less-experienced classmates by modeling professional practice, giving specific and constructive task feedback, checking work against shared standards, and helping teams maintain quality. They practice leadership under teacher supervision; they do not replace the teacher, serve as formal instructors, or manage the class.

Capstone event

Mercantile pop-up and pathway culmination

Kitchen Management students would help plan and execute the pathway's culminating Mercantile pop-up/capstone event. Responsibilities may include concept development, recipe testing, menu design, costing, production planning, scheduling, delegation, quality control, service planning, and post-event evaluation.

Students would first practice leadership within their own course teams. They would later demonstrate leadership during preparation and execution of the capstone event, connecting culinary skill, enterprise work, leadership, and career readiness.

Status: Final event structure, timing, title, scope, and responsibility need Jason/Corey confirmation.

Boundaries

Teacher authority stays clear

Expanded student leadership requires clear boundaries. Students may practice leadership, delegation, communication, and quality control, but the teacher remains responsible for instruction, safety, grading, discipline, accommodations, finances, legal requirements, and emergency response.
