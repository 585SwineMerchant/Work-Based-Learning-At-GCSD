Explore step 5

Leadership and Kitchen Management

Kitchen Management is the leadership bridge: students keep doing culinary work while learning how to plan, lead, coach, and evaluate production.

No false choice

Leadership still includes cooking

Recipe testing and skill maintenance

Students continue culinary practice while testing recipes, refining procedures, and keeping core production skills sharp.

Menu and event planning

Students may work on menu development, costing, ordering needs, scheduling, delegation, and event preparation.

Quality control and coaching

Students practice giving clear direction, checking work, debriefing results, and supporting younger or less experienced classmates.

Subject to confirmation

Capstone responsibility

The pathway materials connect Kitchen Management to leadership responsibility and a possible capstone event. Public copy should keep that connection restrained until Jason/Corey confirm the exact event, location, calendar, staffing, and student responsibilities.

Boundaries

Teacher authority stays clear

  • The teacher remains responsible for safety, grading, discipline, accommodations, legal requirements, finances, and emergencies.
  • Student leadership means increased responsibility, not independent authority over classmates or program decisions.
  • Travel Rights should mean readiness to represent the program beyond the classroom, not only eligibility for senior WBL.
