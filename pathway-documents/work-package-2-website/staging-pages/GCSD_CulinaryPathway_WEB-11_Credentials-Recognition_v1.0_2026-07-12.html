Credentials & Recognition

Credentials and Recognition

Eight related decisions, kept separate on purpose so no single record or score is used beyond what it actually proves.

Several distinct credentials, assessments, and recognitions touch the culinary program. They occur near similar points in a student's progression, but each answers a different question and is recorded separately.

Three Decisions, One Transition Point

Gateway 5 completion, technical-assessment completion, and CTE endorsement / 4+1 recognition may be reviewed together for efficiency near the Grade 11 transition, but they remain separate judgments — one record never silently substitutes for another.

No local passing score, retest rule, or fee/accommodation policy is stated on this page. Current provider and NYSED requirements control and must be verified before each administration cycle.

Active

ServSafe Management

Provider: National Restaurant Association (or current approved provider).

Answers: Has the student earned an industry-recognized food-safety credential?

Evidence: Approved provider written exam result. Timing: Advanced Culinary (Unit 2 ServSafe Exam, per current practice); reviewed again at the Gateway 4/5 transition.

Does not independently prove: safe daily behavior, sanitation routines, allergen control, equipment use, or professional judgment — direct observation remains required.

ServSafe Management is a current credentialing option for culinary students. Current provider exam edition, passing score, retest rule, and accommodation policy control — not locally invented.

Curriculum-Embedded Assessment & Recognition

Six additional items, distinguished by purpose

These items are woven into the existing and proposed course sequence. GOV-10 does not carry an individual authorization status entry for each one separately from the courses that teach them; overall pathway status is tracked under the program-architecture and Gateway-framework entries (see Pathway at a Glance).

YouScience Culinary III (Technical Assessment)

Provider: YouScience (or current approved provider) — pathway technical-assessment blueprint.

Answers: Has the student completed the current approved technical-assessment components and achieved the required result?

Evidence: Written domain results (11 weighted standards) plus performance-expectation results, distributed across all four pathway courses, with a terminal administration near the Gateway 5 transition.

Does not independently prove: Gateway 5, Travel Rights, or full pathway competence by itself — a single total score may not overrule a larger body of current evidence unless policy explicitly requires it.

ProStart Level 1

Provider: National Restaurant Association Educational Foundation (NRAEF).

Answers: Has the student covered the national restaurant/foodservice curriculum framework for foundational chapters?

Evidence: Curriculum coverage across Culinary 1, Culinary 2, and Advanced Culinary.

Does not independently prove: mastery by itself — curriculum coverage alone does not prove mastery or award a credential. Any postsecondary-credit implication is governed by the current New York State ProStart Articulation Agreement, which must be verified before advising a student.

ProStart Level 2

Provider: National Restaurant Association Educational Foundation (NRAEF).

Answers: Has the student covered the advanced national curriculum framework (breakfast, nutrition, cost control, salads, purchasing, proteins, marketing, desserts, sustainability, global cuisine)?

Evidence: Curriculum coverage across Culinary 2, Advanced Culinary, and the proposed Kitchen Management course. Two Global Cuisine domains are currently only partially represented in existing course content — a flagged, unresolved curriculum gap, not papered over.

Does not independently prove: mastery by itself; does not replace approved curriculum or assessment tools.

CDOS (Career Development and Occupational Studies)

Provider: New York State Education Department (NYSED).

Answers: Is the student developing observable career-development, employability, and occupational-learning evidence?

Evidence: Employability profile ratings across 11 work-related-skill domains, plus Gateway evidence, continuous across the full course sequence.

Does not independently prove: professionalism as a single teacher's impression — CDOS grounds it in observable evidence. Current NYSED CDOS commencement-credential pathway requirements must be confirmed with counseling before certifying.

Pathway Technical Assessment (terminal result)

Administered by: the district, using the YouScience Culinary III blueprint.

Answers: Has the student completed the current approved technical-assessment components and achieved the required result?

Evidence: Combined written and performance-expectation result, near the Gateway 5 / Grade 11–12 transition.

Does not independently prove: Gateway 5 or CTE endorsement by itself — it remains a separate judgment. A single total score does not overrule a larger current body of evidence unless current approved policy explicitly requires it.

CTE Endorsement / 4+1 Recognition

Governing bodies: New York State Education Department (NYSED); district diploma process.

Answers: Has the student met all current state and district requirements for the formal pathway outcome?

Evidence: Current approved pathway, assessment, course, portfolio, and credit records, confirmed at the Grade 11–12 transition and finalized at graduation.

Does not independently prove: is not a separate Gateway and is not itself an assessment method. Current authorized NYSED and district requirements control and must be verified before each administration cycle.

Approved Postsecondary Credit (potential 4+1 / dual enrollment)

Provider: a partner postsecondary institution, per current articulation agreement.

Answers: Has the student earned transferable postsecondary credit tied to a current approved articulation agreement?

Evidence: Postsecondary course completion and credit record, typically confirmed at or after the Gateway 4/5 transition.

Does not independently prove: automatic credit at every institution — articulation agreements are institution-specific and time-limited. The current New York State ProStart Articulation Agreement (or successor agreement) must be verified before advising a specific credit outcome.

No single score decides everything

None of the items above, taken alone, substitutes for the others or for the Culinary Passport's full body of evidence. A credential result, a curriculum-coverage record, and a competency decision answer different questions and are kept in different records on purpose. See Six Gateways and the Culinary Passport for how competency itself is verified.
