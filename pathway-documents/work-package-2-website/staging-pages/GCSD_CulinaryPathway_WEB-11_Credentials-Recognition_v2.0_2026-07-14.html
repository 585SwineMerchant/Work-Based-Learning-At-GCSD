Credentials, College Credit, and Graduation

Students and families need to know what can be earned, what may support graduation planning, and what still needs confirmation.

What matters

Four reader questions

What can I earn?

ServSafe is a current credentialing option. Other credentials and assessments must be stated using verified provider and district language.

What can support graduation?

The district pathway presentation connects Culinary Arts with national assessment, possible 4+1 use, and Career and Technical endorsement planning.

What may carry college credit?

College-credit opportunities may exist through current agreements, but exact MCC equivalencies should not be published until verified.

What can I put on a resume?

Credentials, course completion, enterprise experience, leadership roles, and verified workplace evidence each have different resume value.

Evidence types

Related, not interchangeable

  • Course credit shows successful completion of a district course.
  • A credential shows performance on a provider or industry-recognized measure.
  • Gateway evidence shows readiness for a specific level of responsibility within the pathway framework.
  • Diploma recognition and 4+1 planning depend on current district and NYSED requirements for each student.
Conservative claims

Language held for confirmation

Exact MCC course mappings, official Portrait of a Graduate language, assessment passing rules, retest policies, accommodation procedures, and student-specific graduation eligibility should be confirmed before publication.
