Credentials, College Credit, and Graduation

Culinary coursework can support credentials, college credit, diploma planning, and resume evidence when students meet the relevant course, assessment, and program requirements.

Credentials and assessments

What students may earn or complete

ServSafe

Advanced Culinary includes access to the ServSafe Management Exam. ServSafe is a food-safety credential opportunity tied to documented course preparation.

YouScience Culinary Arts assessment

The pathway uses a YouScience Culinary Arts technical assessment opportunity as part of credential and recognition planning. Exact administration details should be confirmed before publication.

ProStart

The Advanced Culinary course is aligned with the National Restaurant Association Educational Foundation/ProStart curriculum. Exact ProStart level, exam, certificate, and articulation language require district confirmation.

CDOS

NYSED recognizes Career Development and Occupational Studies pathways and the CDOS Commencement Credential. Culinary coursework and supervised WBL may support CDOS planning when all NYSED and district requirements are met.

Graduation pathways

CTE, CDOS, and the 4+1 option

NYSED describes a Career and Technical Education pathway, a CDOS pathway, and the CDOS Commencement Credential. For the CTE pathway, NYSED states that students must meet diploma requirements, pass four required Regents exams, successfully complete a NYSED-approved CTE program of study, and pass each part of the three-part technical assessment. NYSED also identifies approved CTE technical assessments as usable for the fifth assessment in the 4+1 pathway when tied to an approved program.

These opportunities depend on the student's transcript, program completion, assessment completion, and district reporting. Course credit, credentials, competency evidence, college credit, diploma pathways, and resume evidence are connected, but they are not the same record.

Official references: NYSED CTE/CDOS pathway requirements; NYSED CTE Program Approval; NYSED graduation requirements FAQ.

College credit

Current opportunity, mappings pending

College-credit opportunities currently exist for Culinary Arts and Nutrition I, Culinary Arts and Nutrition II, and Advanced Culinary Arts.

The exact Monroe Community College course equivalencies, course titles, credits, eligibility requirements, registration procedures, transcript procedures, and current articulation language still require confirmation before the site publishes course-by-course mappings.

Profile of a Graduate

A broad district vision

GCSD's Profile of a Graduate describes a broad district vision for the capabilities and attributes students should develop for life after graduation. Culinary Arts supports that vision through communication, collaboration, problem-solving, responsibility, adaptability, professional conduct, and preparation for career and postsecondary choices.

The Profile is not presented here as a course requirement, credential checklist, or one-to-one evidence map. Exact district vocabulary and any formal Culinary Pathway connection still require confirmation.

Resume and portfolio value

What evidence can show

Students may use verified coursework, credentials, college-credit participation, enterprise experience, leadership assignments, assessed labs, event reflection, and portfolio artifacts to describe what they have practiced and demonstrated. Resume language should stay accurate to what the student actually completed.
