Proposed Culmination — Grade 12 Conditional — Not Authorized

External Work-Based Learning

The proposed senior-year course that would let a student apply three years of Culinary Pathway learning in a real, supervised workplace — not currently authorized, and not available to any student today.

External Work-Based Learning is the proposed final piece of the three-year Culinary Pathway, not the whole pathway. Everything on this page describes what a proposed Grade 12 course would involve if the district authorizes it in the future. It is not a description of a course any student currently takes, is enrolled in, or can complete today.

Program Status — Read Before Citing (GOV-10 PA-06)

Conditional planning draft. Not adopted district policy. Not authorized for implementation. Structures, templates, workflows, course framework, approval requirements, and launch-readiness materials may be drafted now; legal, labor-law, insurance, transportation, staffing, fiscal, NYSED-registration, and district-policy claims remain unverified and must not be finalized without current official sources.

This exact status statement applies to every section on this page, without exception. Look for the repeated Conditional label as you read.

Course Overview

The proposed capstone of the Culinary Pathway

Conditional — Not Authorized

External Work-Based Learning would be the proposed Grade 12 culmination of the three-year Culinary Pathway sequence — it is one course at the end of that sequence, not a description of the whole pathway. Under the proposed design, a student would arrive at this course only after progressing through Culinary 1 and Culinary 2, Advanced Culinary and The Cottage, and Kitchen Management. See the Courses & Learning Environments page and the Pathway at a Glance page for how the full three-year sequence fits together.

As proposed, this would be a 1.0-credit course supervised by a credentialed Work-Based Learning Coordinator — a role not currently staffed — combining classroom-based coordination with supervised practice at an approved training station. It is conditional planning draft. Not adopted district policy. Not authorized for implementation.

Eligibility

Travel Rights would be the prerequisite, not a given

Conditional — Not Authorized

Under the proposed design, no student could begin External Work-Based Learning without first completing Gateway 5 (Professional, Leadership, and Career Readiness) inside the school program — the readiness students would earn through the proposed Kitchen Management course, referred to in everyday language as Travel Rights. See the Leadership & Kitchen Management page for the full Travel Rights concept.

Travel Rights would confirm educational readiness only. Earning it would not guarantee a specific employer, schedule, transportation arrangement, support plan, or placement, and it would not by itself authorize a student to begin this course — that would still require the course itself to be district-authorized.

Gateway 6

The Passport's final Gateway — still a conditional planning draft

Conditional — Not Authorized

Gateway 6 (Completed Authentic Workplace Learning) is the proposed Culinary Passport Gateway connected to this course. It would be verified solely by the credentialed Work-Based Learning Coordinator, representing the transfer of three years of pathway competency into professional practice. See the Six Gateways & Culinary Passport page for the full Gateway framework.

Under the proposed design, Gateway 6 would require, together:

  • Gateway 5 / Travel Rights completed before the course begins.
  • An approved training station and an individualized training plan already in place.
  • A proposed 300 verified workplace hours.
  • A completed portfolio, including individualized placement evidence.
  • An employer evaluation.
  • A coordinator evaluation.
  • A final presentation or exit interview.
  • Acceptable safety, integrity, and professional conduct, with no unresolved serious program violation.

Hours and evidence would both be required — a strong portfolio could not replace missing workplace hours, and 300 hours could not replace a completed portfolio and required evaluation evidence.

Conditional Planning Draft

Conditional planning draft. Not adopted district policy. Not authorized for implementation. Gateway 6 cannot currently be completed by any student. The Culinary Passport's Gateway 6 tab exists so the record structure is ready, but no Gateway 6 completion may be recorded while this authorization remains conditional.

Hours

A proposed 300-hour framework, not a current requirement

Conditional — Not Authorized

Under the proposed design, a placement would be planned around a benchmark of roughly 10 hours per week across approximately 30 active workweeks of the course year, working toward a total of 300 verified hours by the end of the course. This is described as a planning benchmark, not a fixed weekly requirement — an individual student's schedule would flex around school attendance, other commitments, and a workplace's actual needs.

Specific permitted-hours limits for a given student, by age and school-day status, are not stated on this page and would need to be verified against current minor-employment law before any schedule is finalized.

Individualized Planning

A student-specific training plan, before the first counted hour

Conditional — Not Authorized

Before a placement's first counted hour, the proposed design calls for a written, individualized training plan for each student, connecting the placement to that student's specific learning objectives, career goal, and demonstrated pathway competencies. The coordinator would set the objectives and required documentation; the training station would contribute site-specific detail such as schedule, supervision arrangement, and approved duties. The student, family (where applicable), mentor, and coordinator would all sign before any hours begin.

The plan would also identify the specific evidence expected from that placement, beyond the common assignments every student completes, and would set fixed points across the placement where progress is reviewed together.

Employer Supervision

A workplace mentor, working from an approved plan

Conditional — Not Authorized

Division of Authority. Under the proposed model, an employer would not design curriculum. The educator — the credentialed Work-Based Learning Coordinator, in coordination with the Culinary Pathway teaching team — would set learning objectives and required documentation. A workplace mentor would supervise the student's daily work, communicate regularly with the coordinator, observe performance, and give feedback within the approved duties and training plan.

Full detail on the mentor's role, what would make a quality training station, and how a business or organization would partner is on the Employer & Community Partners page.

Coordinator Monitoring

Ongoing oversight, not a one-time approval

Conditional — Not Authorized

Under the proposed design, a credentialed Work-Based Learning Coordinator would stay involved throughout a placement, not only at the start. That would include regular check-ins with the student, regular contact with the mentor, and site visits at key points in the placement — an early visit, a midpoint visit, and a final visit, plus any additional visit a concern required — to confirm the student is present, supervised, safe, and working within approved duties.

A concern about the placement, or about a student's performance, would be handled by the coordinator directly. A weak training-station condition would not be recorded as a student's failure, and a student performance concern would not automatically be treated as a site failure — the two would remain separate determinations even when they arise from the same event.

Portfolio

A common portfolio, plus placement-specific evidence

Conditional — Not Authorized

Under the proposed design, every student would complete the same sequence of common portfolio assignments — for example, a placement and workplace-safety orientation reflection, a communication and teamwork reflection, a workplace problem-solving case, a quality-control analysis, a supervisor or mentor interview, and a final career-fit reflection — so every student's portfolio follows a consistent structure. The individualized training plan would add placement-specific evidence on top of that common core, so the portfolio reflects each student's actual placement without forcing unlike workplaces into one identical model.

Every Day Is a School Day. The portfolio would be designed to capture learning from ordinary shifts, changing responsibilities, feedback, mistakes, and everyday problem solving — not only from special projects.

Evaluation

Two sources of evidence, one final decision

Conditional — Not Authorized

A workplace mentor would provide direct, periodic feedback on a student's performance through an employer evaluation — important evidence, but not the final word. The credentialed Work-Based Learning Coordinator would remain solely responsible for the final, standards-based course evaluation and the Gateway 6 decision; employer feedback would inform that decision without determining it by itself. The student would also contribute a self-evaluation as part of the complete body of evidence.

Closing the Placement

Reflection and a final presentation

Conditional — Not Authorized

Near the end of a placement, the proposed design calls for a student to complete a final reflection connecting hours, evidence, and feedback to a realistic next step — further education, direct employment, or another opportunity — and to present that reflection, or complete an exit interview, as part of the final evaluation.

Status Reminder

To repeat: External Work-Based Learning is a conditional planning draft. Not adopted district policy. Not authorized for implementation. None of the eligibility, Gateway 6, hours, training-plan, supervision, monitoring, portfolio, evaluation, or presentation content described above is currently available to any student.

Several dependencies remain unresolved before this course could move beyond a planning draft, including course authorization, staffing and credentialing for a Work-Based Learning Coordinator, transportation, insurance, labor-law, fiscal, and state-registration questions, and applicable district policy. These are tracked in the Production Control Log's Legal-Policy Verification tab and Work Package 6's Legal-Policy Dependency Log, and must be confirmed from current official sources — not from this page — before any of them can be finalized.
