Courses and Learning Environments

What students actually take, in what order, and what each course establishes.

Culinary 1, Culinary 2, and Advanced Culinary are existing GCSD courses, described here in full detail. The Cottage, Kitchen Management, SkillsUSA, and the proposed senior Work-Based Learning course are covered only briefly below — detailed pages for those are still in development.

Grade 10

Culinary 1 and Culinary 2

Active

Active — existing GCSD instructional program; document title and course-sequence terminology pending final alignment with the official district course guide.

Culinary 1 and Culinary 2 remain two distinct, separately scheduled courses, but their evidence forms one developmental foundation. A 35-week outline, organized into nine instructional units with a repeated 3-day lab cycle (about 20% of each lab day dedicated to cleanup and station preparation), maps onto Gateways 1 through 3.

Gateway 1 — safety and sanitation (Weeks 1–3, reinforced Weeks 34–35)

  • Week 1 — Overview of Kitchen Safety & Hygiene; Personal Hygiene and Workplace Behavior (handwashing, clothing/hair restraints, glove use, designated eating/drinking areas)
  • Week 2 — Food Flow & Temperature Control; Preventing Contamination & Safe Storage (HACCP, FIFO, FAT TOM, refrigerator/freezer temperatures, contamination types, pathogens)
  • Week 3 — Knife Identification & Safety; Kitchen Tools, Measuring & Mise en Place

Gateway 2 — kitchen-work readiness (Units 2–8, Weeks 4–25)

Built directly into the lab structure rather than one single unit: Day 2 of every lab cycle is mise en place and station organization; Days 1 and 3 require reading and executing standard recipes, managing time, and closing/cleaning the station. Week 24 (Effective Communication & Teamwork) makes team communication explicit content.

Gateway 3 — foundational culinary competence (Weeks 26–35)

  • Weeks 26–28 — Final Project Preparation & Research: project selection, planning, costing, market order, presentation criteria
  • Weeks 29–31 — Final Project Execution (planning/cooking/plating structure)
  • Weeks 32–33 — Final Project Presentations & Feedback
  • Weeks 34–35 — Comprehensive Review, Written Final Exam, Practical Final Exam / Skills Demonstration

External alignment: YouScience Service Equipment, Sanitation & Food Safety, and Math Concepts standards; ProStart Level 1 chapters on Keeping Food Safe, Workplace Safety, Kitchen Essentials, and Communication; CDOS Safety, Workplace Appearance, Ethical/Legal Responsibilities, Attendance, Punctuality, Quality of Work, Resource Management, and Interpersonal Dynamics. See Credentials and Recognition for how these are distinguished from a completed credential.

Grade 11

Advanced Culinary

Active

Active — existing GCSD instructional program; document title and course-sequence terminology pending final alignment with the official district course guide.

Advanced Culinary moves from foundational technique to client-centered performance, and establishes Gateway 4. It is organized into seven units running September through June, and is realized in the school-based enterprise environment known as The Cottage (see brief note below).

  • Unit 1 (September) — Introduction to the Food/Hospitality Industry; Career Research Project
  • Unit 2 (October) — Kitchen Operations: Food Safety, Workplace Safety, Kitchen Basics, Knife Skills; includes the ServSafe Exam and a Knife Skills food lab
  • Unit 3 (October) — Professionalism in the Kitchen (Service & Management); a Hors d'oeuvre Reception food lab
  • Unit 4 (Nov/Dec) — Intro to Baking; Pop-Up Event 1: CTE Night at the Greece Performing Arts Center
  • Unit 5 (Jan/Feb) — Cooking Techniques Part 1 (stocks, soups, sauces, vegetables, starches, salads, sandwiches, pizza); Pop-Up Event 2: Titan Bake Sale
  • Unit 6 (Mar/Apr) — Cooking Techniques Part 2 (poultry, meat, seafood); Pop-Up Event 3 — status Planned/Unconfirmed in the current source document
  • Unit 7 (May/June) — Cooking Techniques Part 3 (dairy and eggs); Pop-Up Event 4: Teacher Appreciation Week breakfast preparation

External alignment: ProStart service, management, cost control, purchasing, marketing, nutrition, and production topics; YouScience performance expectations; CDOS work-related skills.

A successful pop-up event does not, by itself, prove that every student completed Gateway 4. Client satisfaction, revenue, and public visibility describe the event; none of them independently proves individual student learning.

Proposed

The Cottage

The Cottage is the Culinary Pathway's proposed name and instructional framework for GCSD's existing in-house catering and school-based enterprise activity — it is not simply a catering business, but an authentic, client-centered learning environment connected to Gateway 4. The underlying catering and enterprise activity already exists at GCSD. See the The Cottage page for full detail.

Proposed

Kitchen Management

Proposed planning document. Course title, sequence, credit, staffing, and implementation status require district confirmation. Kitchen Management is not currently an active, authorized, or district-confirmed course. Its purpose is not additional cooking instruction — it is the leadership bridge between culinary production and professional employment, connected to Gateway 5. A proposed scope and sequence exists as a planning document; see the Leadership and Kitchen Management page for full detail.

Exploratory

SkillsUSA development initiative

Exploratory / interest-development stage — active chapter status is not confirmed. The Culinary Pathway may discuss possible future participation, student interest, informational sessions, and competition opportunities under the name "SkillsUSA development initiative." GCSD does not currently state or imply that an active SkillsUSA chapter operates.
